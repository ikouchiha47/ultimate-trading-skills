## 2. Business description
Canara Bank — incorporated 1906, nationalised 1969, **merged with Syndicate Bank (Apr 2020)**;
HQ Bangalore (sourced). Full-service PSU bank: retail / wholesale / treasury / life-insurance
segments. Key quality ratios **as of Q3 FY26 (sourced)**: NIM **2.50%**, **GNPA 2.08%**, **NNPA
0.45%**, CASA **29.52%**, PCR **94.19%**, Cost-to-Income **46.86%**, Cost of Funds **5.18%**. The
GNPA/NNPA + 94% PCR is the clean-balance-sheet story; NIM 2.5% is the PSU-typical thin spread.

## 3. Industry & competitive positioning
See `00_industry.md` (Porter / PESTEL). CANBK is a mid-large PSU bank; competes on reach + sovereign
trust, lags private banks on NIM. Moat = deposit franchise + branch network, not pricing power.

## 4. Investment summary
**Re-rated cyclical recovery, now consolidating.** 5-yr profit CAGR **44%**, stock CAGR **33%**,
1-yr **+17%** (sourced) — a strong run. Valuation still modest (P/E 6.9, P/B 1.05). Recent
developments (sourced): **new MD & CEO Brajesh Kumar Singh (eff. 1 Jun 2026)**; board approved a
**FY26-27 capital-raising plan** (watch dilution); **RBI imposed a ₹41.80 lakh penalty (5 Jun
2026)** for KYC/inoperative-account lapses (small, reputational not financial). **Mispricing
thesis:** still below the re-rating-toward-book that asset-quality cleanup supports, *if* NIM holds
and capital raise isn't dilutive at a discount. Quarterly Net Profit is **lumpy** (₹5111→3233→4896
→5174→**4575** Cr last 5 q (sourced)) — momentum flattening, not breaking.
