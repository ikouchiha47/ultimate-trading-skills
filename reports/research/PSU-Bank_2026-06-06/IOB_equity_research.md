## 1. Investment summary
**Optically growing, actually de-rating at a premium.** FY26 (concall, sourced): net profit
**₹5,418 Cr (+56% YoY)**, NII ₹11,916 Cr (+37%), global advances +24.16%, deposits +18.03%.
**The trap:** the +56% profit growth is a low-base effect (FY21 net profit ₹758 Cr); the stock
fell **−18.8% over 1-yr** — the market is pricing a quality/risk discount, not rewarding earnings
growth. At **P/E 11.7, P/B 1.71** (richest in the cohort) with a **falling 200-DMA**, the stock
offers the worst risk/reward in the basket. **Positive factors:** asset quality is genuinely clean
(GNPA 1.42%, NNPA 0.21%), NIM 3.21% is second-best, and retail/agri loan growth is explosive.
But the premium multiple gives no margin of safety.

## 3. Industry forces → how they hit IOB (sector analysis applied)
*(The sector frameworks live in [00_industry](00_industry.md); here is how each maps to THIS bank.)*

- **Porter — supplier power (funding):** IOB's **CASA 40.99%** is the second-best in the cohort
  (behind MAHABANK's 52.5%) — a genuine strength. The retail deposit franchise (retail term 53%)
  is well-developed. **NIM 3.21%** is the second-best, making IOB the least constrained on funding
  cost among the worst-performing names.
- **Porter — buyer power / rivalry:** RAM book at 83% of domestic advances is an outlier-positive
  — retail/agri/MSME lending carries better spreads than corporate. The corporate lending portion
  (17%) is modest, reducing rivalry pressure.
- **PESTEL — rates:** Higher NIM provides more buffer than peers; the 56% profit surge was partly
  NIM-driven (expanded from `unknown` to 3.21%). Rate cuts would further benefit the floating-rate
  RAM book.
- **PESTEL — policy/ownership:** GoI holds **92.44%** (sourced, Mar 2026) — the **highest promoter
  holding in the cohort** → minimal free float, directed-lending overhang, and the ₹5,000 Cr capital
  raise (largely GoI-subscribed) is dilutive to minority holders. The IFSCA GIFT City licence (Jun
  2026) is a small positive for diversifying income.
- **RBI sectoral deployment (system):** IOB's RAM tilt (agri 35%, retail 31%, MSME ~17%) aligns
  with system-credit tailwinds but agri lending carries climatic/PSL risk.
- **Influence graph (computed):** Market-beta-dominated like all PSU banks. IOB's thin free float
  (7.56%) amplifies price moves on low volume (RelVol 0.42×) — meaning any serious buying/selling
  from DIIs/FIIs (DIIs 4.28%, FIIs 0.43%) can produce outsized moves.
- **Strategy (computed, EARNED):** Below a **falling 200-DMA** (the EARNED strategy's trend filter)
  there is **no reversion edge**. The sector's 50-DMA mean-reversion play is only valid above the
  200-DMA. IOB is in a structural downtrend — stand aside.
