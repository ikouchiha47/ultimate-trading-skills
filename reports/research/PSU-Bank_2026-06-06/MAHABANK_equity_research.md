## 2. Business description
Full-service PSU bank. **Revenue mix (sourced): Retail ~46% (Q3 FY26 vs ~35% FY22), Corporate/
Wholesale ~35% (vs ~30%), Treasury ~19%** — a deliberate, sustained **shift toward retail** lending
(the highest-quality growth market per RBI). Deposits ₹3,50,538 Cr, Investments ₹1,01,588 Cr,
Borrowing ₹35,234 Cr (lowest leverage; Mar 2026, sourced).

## 3. Industry & positioning
Smallest by mcap of the 10 but the **best operator** — highest ROE (22.7%) by a wide margin. See `00_industry.md`.

## 4. Investment summary
Operationally the standout: **ROE 22.7%, 5-yr profit CAGR 65%, TTM +27%**, 1-yr **+39.8%**.
Quarterly Net Profit strongly rising ₹1,669→1,799→**2,045 Cr** (sourced). The retail-mix shift is a
genuine quality story. The ONLY problem is the entry price (§7).

## 5. Valuation
P/E 8.68, **P/B 1.83 (richest)** — but with ROE 22.7% the premium is earned on fundamentals. The
issue is technical extension, not the multiple per se. DCF `unknown`.

## 6. Financial analysis
Best-in-cohort profitability + a strengthening retail franchise + lowest leverage. The 65% 5-yr CAGR
is partly base effect but the ROE trend is real. Loan mix sourced (retail-led, above).

## 7. Price & flow
`charts/MAHABANK_price_volume.png`. Computed: **+23.7% above 200-DMA and +5.1% above 50-DMA =
extended**, 1-yr **+39.8%**, but **volume falling 0.60×** and **absorption 0.04 (lowest — no fresh
accumulation)**. *A vertical move running out of fuel. The EARNED 50-DMA strategy explicitly buys
PULLBACKS to the 50-DMA, not extension — so this is a hold/wait, not a chase.*

## 8. Risks
Stretched price = drawdown risk on any wobble; richest P/B = most to lose on a de-rating; small/less
liquid. No qualified opinion sourced.
